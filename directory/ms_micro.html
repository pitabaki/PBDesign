2014

Holiday Video

Objective

To offer up an inclusive “Season’s Greetings” (“Happy Holidays”) with out directly calling out, or referencing, any particular holiday. Attempt to cover a diverse ethos within a short period of time — the animation should be more than 20 seconds but no more than 40.

Proposed Color Palette

Keyframes

On the left are keyframes. Essentially, these represent what I’d like the overall look and feel to be for each scene. As of now, only three of these four scenes have backgrounds, with only one of them (the final scene) being fully developed. This merely gives you an idea of what the animation will look like.

Themes

I’d like to cover as many regions as possible within a short period of time. With that goal in mind, I’ll be using a color palette built with both warm and cool colors — a warm color palette will allow for inclusion of the Southern Hemisphere, while the cool color palette will account for the Northern.

top↑

Animation

To transition from frame to frame, the triangle shapes will collapse in on themselves, then rebuild themselves in the next scene as another object (e.g. tree, branch, etc).

Here, the branch collapses in on itself as its flowers either wilt or shed their pedals. The falling pedal then changes color and transforms into the tree in the next scene. Meanwhile, the text that was brushed in is now being erased.

The pedal from the previous scene builds itself into a pine tree. While that’s happening, the mountain in the background sprouts up, and the drop of rain water forms the lake behind the tree.
